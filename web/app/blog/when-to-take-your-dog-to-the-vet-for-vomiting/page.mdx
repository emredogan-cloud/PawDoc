# When to Take Your Dog to the Vet for Vomiting

_Last updated May 28, 2026 · General information, not veterinary advice._

Most dogs vomit occasionally, and a single episode after scarfing grass or a new
treat is rarely cause for alarm. But vomiting can also be the first sign of
something serious. Here's how to tell the difference — and when to stop watching
and call your vet.

## Call a vet right away if you notice any of these

These can signal an emergency. Contact your veterinarian or an emergency clinic
**immediately** if your dog has:

- **Repeated vomiting** (more than 2–3 times in a few hours) or it won't stop
- **Unproductive retching** or a swollen, hard belly (a possible bloat emergency)
- **Blood** in the vomit, or material that looks like coffee grounds
- **Lethargy, collapse, pale gums, or trouble breathing**
- Signs of **pain**, a known **toxin** or foreign-object ingestion, or
- Vomiting in a **puppy, senior, or dog with a known illness**

## Usually OK to monitor at home for a short while

A single vomit in an otherwise bright, playful, well-hydrated adult dog is often
self-limiting. You can briefly monitor if your dog:

- Vomited **once**, then went back to normal
- Is **alert, drinking, and not in pain**
- Has **no** diarrhea, blood, or belly swelling

Offer water in small amounts, hold food for a few hours, then reintroduce a small
bland meal. **If anything worsens or new symptoms appear, call your vet.**

## When in doubt, get it checked

You know your dog. If something feels off, it's always reasonable to call your
veterinarian — they would rather hear from you early.
